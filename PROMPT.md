# Original design prompt

The prompt used to generate the first (broad, visionary) draft. Note: the deliverables in this repo have since been **re-grounded to the actual internal-first "One" strategy** from the brainstorming session (see `README.md` and the vision doc). Keep this prompt as a starting point to re-run or evolve the direction — but treat the meeting decisions as the source of truth.

---

You are a Principal Product Designer with expertise in enterprise SaaS platforms, digital transformation, AI-first user experiences, and design systems.

Design the future UX vision for **One WSO2**, a unified digital experience that brings together every interaction across WSO2 — serving customers, partners, employees, and executives. Instead of separate applications and portals, users have one unified experience where content, applications, workflows, and AI adapt based on who they are.

**Existing experiences** (to converge): Customer Portal, Partner Portal, Employee Intranet, Mobile App.

**Core platform capabilities:** Unified Data Lake, Enterprise Entity Graph, Enterprise Technology Architecture (ETA), Digital Operations platforms (Customer Success Ops, Marketing Ops, Revenue Ops, People Ops, Finance Ops, Digital Ops), and the Novera AI Platform (conversational AI, digital assistant, AI agents, recommendations, workflow automation, enterprise search).

**Design goals:** a visionary yet practical UX for 2026–2027, comparable in quality to Notion, Linear, Slack, GitHub, Microsoft 365, Atlassian, and Salesforce Lightning — while keeping a unique enterprise identity.

Design and describe:

1. **Overall UX Vision** — what the product feels like.
2. **Information Architecture** — navigation for Home, Apps, Workspaces, AI, Notifications, Search, Settings, Profile, and why.
3. **Personalized Home Dashboard** — what each persona sees (Employee, Customer, Partner, Executive): widgets, quick actions, recommendations, insights, AI-generated content.
4. **AI Experience** — how the assistant should appear throughout (persistent sidebar? floating assistant? command palette? contextual assistant? embedded everywhere?). Describe the ideal experience.
5. **Universal Search** — a Google-like enterprise search across people, applications, documents, tickets, customers, products, workflows, AI actions.
6. **Mobile Experience** — daily workflows, approvals, notifications, digital ID, wallet, quick actions, AI.
7. **Navigation Patterns** — left nav, top nav, command palette, breadcrumbs, tabs, contextual actions, with reasoning.
8. **AI-First Interactions** — natural-language workflows, AI-generated dashboards, proactive recommendations, intelligent notifications, workflow suggestions, meeting summaries, personalized daily briefing.
9. **Visual Language** — layout, spacing, typography, iconography, card design, dashboards, color strategy, enterprise design principles. Avoid generic Material Design.
10. **Screens** — detailed UX for Home, Apps Marketplace, Customer Workspace, Employee Workspace, AI Assistant, Universal Search, Notifications Center, Analytics Dashboard, Profile, Mobile Home.
11. **Future Vision** — what One could look like in 2030 if AI agents become the primary way users interact with enterprise software.

**Output format:** UX strategy, user journey diagrams (ASCII fine), navigation hierarchy, wireframe sketches (ASCII), dashboard layouts, component ideas, AI interaction flows, design rationale, enterprise UX best practices.

Think like the lead designer responsible for the next-generation enterprise platform that could compete with Microsoft 365, Salesforce, and Atlassian while remaining simple, elegant, AI-native, and highly productive. Be opinionated, innovative, and detailed.
